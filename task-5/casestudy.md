# Case Study: UPI "Reverse-Pay" Social Engineering Fraud

## 1. Nature of the Crime
UPI-related payment scams are an advanced type of online fraud that do not rely on breaking security systems but instead exploit human behavior. Instead of breaching banking infrastructure, the attacker tricks individuals into approving transactions themselves. This method, known as social engineering, takes advantage of people’s lack of clarity about how UPI works—especially the difference between receiving money and sending it.

## 2. How the Fraud Happens (Step-by-Step)

* **Initial Contact:** The scammer reaches out to the victim through a phone call or messaging platform, pretending to be from a trusted e-commerce company or acting as a buyer on platforms like OLX.  
* **False Claim:** The attacker states that they want to transfer money to the victim, such as a refund or advance payment. They then send a "collect request" or QR code to the victim’s UPI application.  
* **Misleading Instructions:** The victim is told that scanning the QR code and entering their UPI PIN is necessary to receive the money. In reality, this process is designed to withdraw money from their account.
