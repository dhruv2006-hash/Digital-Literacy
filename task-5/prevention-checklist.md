# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

Being digitally aware means staying ahead of online threats. Follow this checklist to keep your digital life secure.

## General Security Practices
1. **Turn on Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Enable 2FA on apps like WhatsApp, Instagram, and Google to add an extra level of protection beyond passwords.  
2. **Be Cautious with Links:** Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links received via SMS or messaging apps that claim prizes or account issues.  
3. **Review App Permissions:** Check your phone settings regularly and restrict access to your camera, microphone, and contacts for apps that don’t truly require them.  
4. **Limit Use of Public Wi-Fi:** Do not access sensitive accounts such as banking or student portals while connected to public Wi-Fi; prefer mobile data.  
5. **Update Your Software:** Keep your device OS and apps updated to ensure you have the latest security fixes.  
6. **Safeguard Personal Documents:** Do not share images of important documents like Aadhaar, PAN, or college ID on social media or with unverified individuals.

## Financial & UPI Safety (India Focus)
7. **Understand the "Receive" Rule:** A UPI PIN is needed only when sending money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to receive funds, it is a scam.  
8. **Confirm Merchant Details:** When paying via QR code, always check that the displayed name matches the actual shop or vendor to avoid fraud.

---

## Reporting Cybercrime
If you become a victim of online fraud or harassment, act quickly:

* **Helpline Number:** Dial **1930** immediately in case of financial fraud.  
* **Official Website:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  

*Note: Taking action within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours) significantly improves the chances of recovering lost money.*
